// Stat Definitions — from The Sports Statistics Manual by Chris Cain
OVR NBA
Overall Rating
A 0–99 composite score based on PPM (Points Per Minute) normalized against the current season's best and worst performers at each position.
PPM NBA
Points Per Minute
Total points scored divided by total minutes played. A fairer comparison than PPG because it accounts for varying playing time. The foundation of the NBA OVR calculation.
PPG+ NBA
Adjusted Points Per Game
PPM × 48 minutes — projects a player's scoring rate over a full 48-minute game regardless of actual minutes played. Allows direct comparison across players with different roles.
PER NBA
Player Efficiency Rating
A per-minute rating that sums up positive contributions (points, rebounds, assists, steals, blocks) and subtracts negatives (turnovers, missed shots) relative to pace.
ORtg NBA
Offensive Rating
Points scored per 100 team possessions. Measures how efficient a team or player is offensively at a possession level.
DRtg NBA
Defensive Rating
Points allowed per 100 team possessions. Lower = better defense. The mirror metric to ORtg.
NET Rtg NBA
Net Rating
ORtg minus DRtg. The single best measure of team quality — the margin of points scored vs. allowed per 100 possessions.
TS% NBA
True Shooting %
PTS ÷ (2 × (FGA + 0.44 × FTA)). Accounts for all ways of scoring including three-pointers and free throws.
eFG% NBA
Effective Field Goal %
(FGM + 0.5 × 3PM) ÷ FGA. Adjusts for the extra value of a made three-pointer.
